THECONSTITUTION OF GLOBAL LEARNING EMPOWERMENT & SCHOLARSHIP FOUNDATION PREAMBLE
...We, the members of GLOBAL LEARNING EMPOWERMENT & SCHOLARSHIP FOUNDATION a not for-profit
...and non-political organization do firmly and solemnly resolve to provide for ourselves a
...constitution
and to be governed by the provisions therein contained. ARTICLE 1: NAME: The Name of the FOUNDATION is “INCORPORATED TRUSTEES OF GLOBAL LEARNING EMPOWERMENT & SCHOLARSHIP FOUNDATION ARTICLE 2: ADDRESS The address of the FOUNDATION shall be situated in Nigeria ARTICLE 3 AIMS & OBJECTIVES
1. To award scholarship & provide learning aids to assist learners/potential learners.
2. To establish, operate and manage schools/ training /entrepreneurship centre of all forms.
3. To organize/launch campaign and advocacy for increase school enrolment girl child education.
4. To renovate improve schools & supply teaching/learning aids (materials) to schools.
5. To sponsor, train & tender support to under-privileged and vulnerable learners (orphans and
vulnerable children-OVCs) in schools.
6. To protect, defend, offer, help and assistance, legal support/ other service, legal support
other services in defense & protection of education of any person as her fundamental human right.
7. To undertake teacher training & capacity building of school personnel for the improvement of
educational quality in the less developed countries of the world. ARTICLE 4: THE TRUSTEES
(A) The Trustees of GLOBAL LEARNING EMPOWERMENT & SCHOLARSHIP FOUNDATION for the purpose of the
Companies and Allied Matters Act No. 1 of 1990 shall be elected at a General Meeting charged with
responsibility of selecting the Trustees with (2/3) majority votes of members present.
(B) Such Trustees (hereinafter referred to as “The Trustees” shall be Seven (7) in number.
(C) The Trustees may hold office for life but shall cease to hold office if he:
(i) Resigns his office
(ii) Ceases to be a member of the registered Trustees of the Body.
(iii) Becomes insane
(iv) Is officially declared bankrupt
(v) Is convicted of a criminal offence involving dishonest by a court of competent jurisdiction
(vi) Is recommended for removal from office by a board of Governors and Trustees majority vote of
members present at any General Meeting of the Body.
(vii) Ceases to reside in Nigeria.
(D) Upon a vacancy occurring in the number of Trustees a General Meeting will be held to appoint
another eligible member of the FOUNDATION. ARTICLE 5: COMMON SEAL
(A) The Trustees shall have a common seal.
(B) Such Common seal will be kept in the custody of the Secretary who shall produce it when
required for use by the Trustees.
(C) All documents to be executed by the Trustees shall be signed by such number of them and
sealed with the common seal ARTICLE 6: MEETINGS 6.1 For effective administration of the FOUNDATION there shall be the following meetings:
1. An Annual General Meeting of the FOUNDATION during the month agreed by Governing Board every
year.
2. Monthly General Meeting - once every month
3. Meeting of the Executive Council
4. Special General Meeting
5. Executive Meeting shall be held twice a month and when the Executive council deem fit, they
may call for a general meeting 6.2 The quorum for the meetings shall respectively be:
1. Notice of Meeting shall be given to the members by the secretary either in person or in
writing.
2. In the case of an Annual general meeting a notice of 21 days shall be given. In case of an
Extra Ordinary General Meeting a notice of 14 days shall be given.
3. A meeting of the FOUNDATION notwithstanding the fact that it is called by shorter notice than
that specified in this regulation shall be deemed to have been duly called if it is so agreed.
4. Resolutions at Annual General Meeting shall be passed by a simple majority of the votes
entitle to be cast by those present at the meeting.
5. No business shall be transacted at any Annual General Meeting unless a quorum of members is
present at the time the meeting proceeds, two-third of the members entitled to attend a meeting
shall constitute a quorum
6. Each member shall have one vote on every resolution ARTICLE 7: GOVERNING BODY
a. They shall formulate policy for the FOUNDATION.
b. They shall direct the opening of the FOUNDATION bank account and to appoint its three (3)
signatories. Any two (2) signatories shall be valid for any withdrawal. One of such signatories
shall be the CHAIRMAN.
c. To receive and consider feed-back on the utilization of grants, scholarship etc., advance by
the FOUNDATION.
d. To examine the merit and demerits of application for grants, and to take appropriate on the
applications. ARTICLE 8: SOURCES OF INCOME.
The sources of income for the FOUNDATION shall include:
1. Accept donations from individuals, Trustees, friends, charity FOUNDATION, corporate bodies and FOUNDATION in Nigeria or elsewhere.
2. Receive grants or assistance from individuals, Trusts, FOUNDATION and other charitable or
philanthropic FOUNDATION in Nigeria or elsewhere.
3. Collect monies and financial assistance from or by any other lawful source or means. ARTICLE 9: DISBURSEMENT AND APPLICATION OF FUNDS.
The funds shall be applied for the purpose of achieving the aims and objectives of the FOUNDATION. ARTICLES 10: KEEPING OF ACCOUNT
The FOUNDATION shall operate bank accounts in its name. The signatories to the account shall the
Chairman, the Financial Secretary and the Treasurer. All monies must be paid into the account
immediately upon receipt and on no account shall the treasurer keep a cash of more than N5,000.00
with him/her at any point in time. The FOUNDATION shall ensure the accurate keeping of record of all income and expenditure. ARTICLE 11: APPOINTMENT OF AUDITOR(S)
1. Independent qualified and licenced Auditors shall be appointed by the general meeting to
audit the financial records of the FOUNDATION annually and submit an audited report to the
Annual General Meeting of the FOUNDATION.
2. The audited financial statements (balance sheet and income and expenditure account) duly
certified by the independent auditors shall be annexed to the annual returns and file with the
Corporate affairs Commission. ARTICLE 12: AMENDMENT OF CONSTITUTION
The FOUNDATION may alter the provision of its Constitution at a General Meeting by a resolution
passed by a simple majority of its members and approved by the Commission. ARTICLE 14: SPECIAL CLAUSE:
1. THE INCOME AND PROPERTY of GLOBAL LEARNING EMPOWERMENT & SCHOLARSHIP FOUNDATION shall be
applied solely towards the promotion of the objects of FOUNDATION as set forth in this RULES
AND REGULATIONS/CONSTITUTION, and no portion thereof shall be paid or transferred directly or
indirectly, by way of dividend, bonus, or otherwise howsoever by way of profit, to the members
of the GLOBAL LEARNING EMPOWERMENT & SCHOLARSHIP FOUNDATION.
2. PROVIDED that nothing herein shall prevent the payment in good faith, or reasonable and
proper remuneration to any officer or servant of the FOUNDATION in return for any service
actually rendered to the FOUNDATION
a. With the exception of ex-officio members of the Governing Council, no member of the
Council of Management or Governing Body shall be appointed to any salaried office of the
FOUNDATION or any office of the FOUNDATION paid by fees; and
b. No remuneration or other benefit in money or money’s worth shall be given by the body to
any member of such Council or Governing Body except repayment of out of pocket expenses or
reasonable and proper rent for premises demised, or let to the FOUNDATION or reasonable fees
for services rendered.
3. If in the event of the winding up or dissolution of the corporate body if there remains
after the satisfaction of all its debts and liabilities, any property whatsoever, the same
shall not be paid to or distributed among the members of the FOUNDATION but shall be given or
transferred to some other institutions, having objects similar to the object of FOUNDATION,
such institutions to be determined by the members of the FOUNDATION at or before the time of
dissolution.
(4) If effect cannot be given to the aforesaid provisions, then he remaining property shall be
transferred to some charitable object.
